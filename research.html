The Small Business Channel of the Matching Multiplier

LSE Economics Department, 2022

This paper investigates whether small businesses serve as a mechanism explaining Patterson's (2021) "matching multiplier," where the pairing of high marginal propensity to consume (MPC) individuals with cyclically sensitive jobs amplifies economic downturns. Using COVID-19 pandemic data, I demonstrate that while this small business channel exists, its effect is insufficient to explain the substantial matching multiplier observed. These findings challenge justifications for macroeconomic stabilization policies specifically targeted at small businesses.

How Does Profit Shifting Affect the Balance of Payments?

IMF Working Paper No. 2021/041

This paper examines how profit shifting by multinational enterprises impacts international financial flows and current account balances. While profit shifting distorts individual components and bilateral balances of the current account, the authors demonstrate that a country's aggregate net balance typically remains unaffected.
